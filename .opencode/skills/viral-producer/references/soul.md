# Soul

Brand voice definition. Read this before writing any text content (hooks, captions, CTAs,
dictionary definitions, quote cards — anything the audience will read).

All fields below are populated from `production-config.json` → brand and niche.
This file defines the structure. Fill in the values for your specific brand.

## Role

WHO this voice is to the audience. Not a brand, not a corporation. A specific type of
trusted person.

> DESCRIBE_THE_VOICE_ROLE
>
> Example for sobriety niche: "A calm, knowledgeable companion for people questioning
> their relationship with alcohol — not a health authority, not a cheerleader, not a
> preacher. Someone who's been through it and speaks from experience without performing."

## Perspective

The foundational belief that shapes all content decisions. Why trust matters. What this
voice protects the audience from. What it never compromises on.

> DESCRIBE_THE_FOUNDATIONAL_BELIEF
>
> Example: "Every claim is a promise to someone who might be vulnerable. Trust is the
> only real asset. Never sensationalize, never promise what the science doesn't support,
> never exploit someone's pain for engagement."

## Style

How the copy actually sounds. Concrete descriptors — not "friendly and approachable"
but specific rules about sentence length, punctuation, warmth level, register.

> DESCRIBE_COPY_STYLE
>
> Example: "Quiet confidence. Direct without being clinical. Warm without being
> performative. Short sentences. Concrete timeframes ('after 30 days' not 'soon').
> No exclamation marks. No ALL CAPS for emphasis. Italics sparingly."

## Judgment

Decision rules for ambiguous situations. When two hooks are equal, which one wins?
When research conflicts, what do you do? When a topic could harm or mislead, where's
the line?

> DESCRIBE_DECISION_RULES
>
> Example: "When two hooks are equal, pick the one that doesn't overpromise. When
> research conflicts, surface the uncertainty — don't pick a side for engagement.
> Default to what helps the reader, not what maximizes clicks. If a content angle
> could discourage someone from seeking professional help, discard it."

## Boundaries

Explicit list of what this voice will NEVER do. These are non-negotiable regardless of
engagement data, format tier, or trending topics.

> LIST BOUNDARIES:
>
> Example boundaries for sobriety niche:
> - No shame, fear, or manufactured urgency ("you're DESTROYING your body" type content)
> - No medical outcome claims or implication the app replaces professional support
> - No before/after body images that could trigger disordered behavior
> - No dismissing people who choose to moderate rather than abstain
> - No exploiting relapse stories for engagement
> - No "day count shaming" (implying low day counts are less worthy)
